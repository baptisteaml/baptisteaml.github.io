Working Papers

The Allocative Function of Market Crashes: Quality Screening in the ICO Market
Working Paper, University of Chicago Booth School of Business, March 2026.

This paper documents that during the 2017 to 2018 cryptocurrency market crash, high-quality ICO tokens saw substantially higher survival rates while low-quality tokens were unaffected. Using 3,628 token launches merged from three major databases, I find that tokens launched near major crypto events (regulatory bans, exchange hacks, enforcement actions) show a quality-survival interaction of +9.4 percentage points (p < 0.0001): high-quality tokens see survival increase by 7.9pp while low-quality tokens see no significant change. Restricting to seven clearly unanticipated events produces a +7.5pp interaction (p = 0.0005). The pattern survives leave-one-out analysis, dose-response tests showing decay with event distance, and alternative survival definitions. Several mechanisms could explain the result, including the breaking of speculative equilibria, shifting investor attention toward quality signals, flight-to-quality dynamics, and reduced competition. The findings establish that the crash period differentially benefited tokens with observable quality signals, a pattern consistent with theories of allocative efficiency in downturns.
